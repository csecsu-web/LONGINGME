About Longingme

A shame-free space to acknowledge what hurts — without being fixed, judged, or analyzed.

What this is

Longingme exists for people who are struggling but not ready for therapy, diagnosis, or labels. It is a space for the in-between — where something is wrong but words for it are still forming.

It is not here to fix you. It is not here to advise you. It is here to hold what you bring to it.

What this is not

Not therapy. Not self-improvement. Not social media. Not a crisis service. Not a productivity tool. Not a place where you need to perform healing.

Privacy

No accounts. No emails. No tracking. Private entries never leave your browser. Shared entries are anonymous text only — nothing else is stored alongside them.

We do not sell data. We do not run ads. We do not share anything. Ever.

Moderation

Content that instructs or encourages self-harm is filtered automatically. You can express pain here. You cannot instruct others to hurt themselves.

Why this exists

The thinking behind Longingme.

The problem

There is a space between "I'm fine" and "I need therapy" where most people live most of the time.

In that space, feelings get suppressed. Shame grows. Things that could have been said in two sentences turn into years of carrying something alone.

Most tools built for this space turn it into performance. Tracking. Streaks. Progress. Optimization. They quietly tell you: you are a problem to be solved.

What we believe

Acknowledging what hurts — just naming it, just saying it — reduces its weight. Not because it fixes anything. But because shame grows in silence, and even one witness changes something.

That witness can be a blank page. It can be the knowledge that someone, somewhere, read your words.

We believe emotional permission is more valuable than emotional optimization.

What we protect

This space is designed to stay simple. We will not add likes, metrics, streaks, AI interpretation, or anything that makes you feel watched or measured.

Every decision goes through one filter: does this increase emotional permission, or does it increase performance pressure?

If it adds pressure — it doesn't get added.

A final thought

Will this cure depression? No.

Will it reduce shame for some people? Very likely.

And reducing shame changes lives more than people realize.

Emotional permission is more valuable than emotional optimization.

You are not broken. You are not too much. You are allowed to be here as you are.
